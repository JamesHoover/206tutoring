Learning How to Learn

Our team of tutors gives lessons in math, reading and writing, and more. But what we’re really teaching our students is how to deal with school: finding out what teachers really want, discovering the discipline to study before a big test, and getting the most out of every lecture and paper. 206 Tutoring specializes in helping students learn how to learn.

When students learn how to learn, they learn not just one subject, but all of them. They become their own teachers, able to judge a context or situation for themselves. They develop their own senses of style and methods for accomplishing their objectives. They free up time and energy to devote to the things that interest them—which are also easier to master when they’ve learned how to learn.

Math

We can teach you a year’s worth of math in under ten hours.

Math is the subject taught worst in school. New teaching methods force students to adopt methods that don’t fit their style. Often, explanations confuse instead of clarifying.

Yet every math class could be aced by a majority of students. Every student deserves to understand math. Math shouldn’t be a cause for anxiety and frustration, but confidence and discovery.

Our methods are personalized, but typically begin with the basics and work their way up, speedily and conceptually, to whatever material the class is covering. This way, the current material is more deeply understood, previous topics will become harder to forget, and upcoming topics will be more swiftly grasped.

Our tutors have experience teaching everything from basic addition to calculus, ages 5 to 45. Several of us were mathletes in middle school, and were two to three years ahead in math in high school. We earned 4’s and 5’s on the Calculus AB, Calculus BC, and Statistics AP tests. Our SAT math scores range from 750 to 800.

"Jacob was an excellent math tutor for material all the way up through the end of pre-calculus. My daughter raised her grade and had more time to devote to her extracurriculars." - John C., Bellevue

Reading and Writing

Mastery of reading and writing is more critical than ever before. College admissions are increasingly competitive and subjective, based on the essay. Jobs are hard to find and harder to get—and your success hinges on résumés, interviews, and communication skills. Academic papers are stressful and difficult.

Whatever your need, 206 Tutoring can help you express yourself.

Writing and reading well are marketable skills. The SAT, ACT, and GRE all have some reading and writing components. Reading others’ ideas and communicating your own clearly will empower you to excel in any private, public, or social situation.

We apply our individualized teaching methods to help students overcome writer’s block, in difficult genres from term papers to college essays, ages 8 and above.
